Users can find numerous free Spider Solitaire games throughout the market. A few versions have developed their individual scoring systems. The scoring system we implement derives from Microsoft's original design standards developed by their programmer team. According to this scoring method all rounds initiate with 500 points. Your points value decreases whenever you use a card for movement. Both dealing from stock and activating the undo function require 1 point each and these steps remain the same at every level. Clearing full sequences of K, Q, J, 10, 9, 8, 7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 2, A cards to the foundation will give you points in the game. Every cleared sequence awards you 100 points under this scoring system.

For example: You finished the game when reaching step 120 of the game. The default 500 points suffer a deduction of the 120 steps played which results in a final score of 380. A game reaches its end when eight sequences become available in the foundation areas. There are eight sequences that each award 100 points which sum to reach 800 points total. The accumulated total of 1180 points results from adding foundation scores of 800 points to initial points of 380 points.

Conclusion: The achievement of a high score in Spider Solitaire requires players to finish the game through the minimum number of moves.
